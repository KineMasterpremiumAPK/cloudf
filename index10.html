Hidden Features in Popular Video Editing Apps You Didn't Know About

Introduction

Video editing apps have become more powerful than ever, offering a range of tools that go beyond basic trimming and effects. However, many users are unaware of the hidden features that can take their editing skills to the next level. In this article, we’ll uncover some of the most overlooked yet powerful features in popular video editing apps.

1. Advanced Color Grading Tools

Most video editors are familiar with basic brightness, contrast, and saturation adjustments, but many apps offer professional-grade color grading features:

  • LUT Support: Apps like Adobe Premiere Pro and DaVinci Resolve allow you to apply Look-Up Tables (LUTs) for cinematic color grading.
  • Selective Color Correction: Fine-tune specific colors in your video to create striking visual effects.

2. Motion Tracking

Motion tracking enables you to follow objects in a video and attach text, effects, or animations to them.

  • How to Use It: In Final Cut Pro, simply select the object and enable tracking to apply effects that move dynamically.
  • Use Cases: Perfect for adding callouts, censoring objects, or enhancing storytelling.

3. Keyframe Animation

Keyframes allow precise control over animations and transitions.

  • Hidden Uses: Animate text, adjust audio levels gradually, and create smooth zoom-ins or pan effects.
  • Available In: Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, HitFilm Express, and even mobile apps like KineMaster.

4. AI-Powered Auto Editing

AI tools can speed up your workflow by automatically cutting, stabilizing, and enhancing videos.

  • Features To Look For: Auto-reframe, smart cropping, and AI-generated subtitles.
  • Top Apps with AI Features: Adobe Premiere Rush, Magisto, and LumaFusion.

5. Advanced Audio Editing and Noise Reduction

Great video content requires clear audio, and many editing apps offer hidden tools to improve sound quality.

  • Noise Reduction: Remove background noise in apps like DaVinci Resolve and Premiere Pro.
  • Audio Ducking: Automatically lower background music when dialogue is detected.

6. Multi-Camera Editing

If you shoot with multiple cameras, multi-cam editing can sync and switch between angles effortlessly.

  • Where To Find It: Final Cut Pro, Premiere Pro, and Davinci Resolve offer built-in multi-cam editing features.
  • Best Use Cases: Interviews, live performances, and action sequences.

7. Green Screen (Chroma Key) Features

Many apps provide advanced chroma keying for professional-looking effects.

  • Hidden Settings: Fine-tune spill suppression and adjust key edges for better blending.
  • Best Apps for Green Screen Editing: iMovie, Kinemaster, and HitFilm Express.

8. 360-Degree Video Editing

For VR and immersive content, 360-degree video editing tools are available in select apps.

  • Features Include: Stitching, stabilization, and directional audio.
  • Top Picks: Adobe Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, and Insta360 Studio.

9. Hidden Export Settings for Better Quality

Most apps allow for customized export settings, but hidden options can drastically improve video quality.

  • Best Settings to Look For: High bitrate exports, HEVC encoding, and lossless compression.
  • Best Apps: HandBrake (for compression), Premiere Pro, and Final Cut Pro.

10. Keyboard Shortcuts for Faster Editing

Editing faster means knowing the best keyboard shortcuts.

  • Popular Shortcuts: Ripple delete, timeline navigation, and quick keyframe adjustments.
  • Apps With Customizable Shortcuts: DaVinci Resolve, Premiere Pro, and Final Cut Pro.

Conclusion

These hidden features in popular video editing apps can help you elevate your content, improve efficiency, and create more professional videos. Take the time to explore these tools in your preferred video editing app—you might be surprised at what’s possible!
